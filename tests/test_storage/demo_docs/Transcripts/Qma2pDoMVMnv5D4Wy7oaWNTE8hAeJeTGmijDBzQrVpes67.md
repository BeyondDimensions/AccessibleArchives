### Stuttgart (dpa) 

In the case of the involvement in several murders of the left-wing extremist RAF, Peter-Jürgen Boock, accused as an accomplice, denied any participation again during the main trial session. **"I am not a murderer. I did not kill any people,"** Boock said after a multi-day interrogation to his defender. Boock is accused of having participated in a RAF commando in shooting at people.

The prosecution accuses Boock as a KGB member of preparing several assassination plans. In engagements at various sites, including the residence of **Hanns-Martin Schleyer** and former Foreign Minister Hans-Dietrich Genscher, Boock allegedly took part in the planning and execution phases. His attendance at the Federal Criminal Police Office in Karlsruhe is being examined.

Boock, who has distanced himself from the RAF, strongly criticizes the union of the Bundeswehr with the peaceful democratic state and accuses the authorities in an open court statement. The final statement of the Karlsruhe lawyers is expected in the proceedings against Boock. 

The documents in the Karlsruhe file indicate an extensive surveillance that followed Boock. Forensic contact with the group of terrorists has been laid out to justify the court demands for life imprisonment. The judgment is expected next Wednesday morning.

**In conclusion**: According to the report, the final evening of the negotiations indicates a surveillance of Boock. Such connections were established by including certain conversations before his arrest. He is directly involved in the 1977 and March 1978 events in Paris.
